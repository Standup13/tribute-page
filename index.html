Simón Bolívar

The Liberator of Latin America


Below is a timeline of Simón Bolívar's life:


  • 1783 - Born on July 24 in Caracas, Venezuela.
  • 1786 - His father, Juan Vicente Bolívar, dies.
  • 1792 - His mother, Concepción Palacios y Blanco, dies.
  • 1799 - Travels to Spain to continue his studies.
  • 1802 - Marries María Teresa Rodríguez del Toro, who dies shortly after.
  • 1805 - Takes the oath on Monte Sacro in Rome to liberate his homeland.
  • 1810 - Joins the Supreme Junta of Caracas that declares the independence of Venezuela.
  • 1813 - Begins the Admirable Campaign and is proclaimed “The Liberator.”
  • 1814 - Defeated at the Battle of La Puerta and exiled to Jamaica.
  • 1819 - Crosses the Andes and wins the Battle of Boyacá, securing the independence of Colombia.
  • 1821 - Victory at the Battle of Carabobo, securing the independence of Venezuela.
  • 1822 - Defeats Spanish forces at the Battle of Pichincha, securing the independence of Ecuador.
  • 1824 - Victory at the Battles of Junín and Ayacucho, securing the independence of Peru.
  • 1825 - Named president of Bolivia.
  • 1826 - Convenes the Congress of Panama to seek the unity of Latin American nations.
  • 1830 - Resigns the presidency of Gran Colombia and dies on December 17 in Santa Marta, Colombia.

“Freedom is the only objective worthy of the sacrifice of human life.”

-- Simón Bolívar
